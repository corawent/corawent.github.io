What makes an efficient solar cell?

High absorption

An efficient solar cell must absorb a lot of sunlight. Each photon from the sun frees an electron to move around in the semiconductor.

Good material quality

Defects in photovoltaic materials can trap free electrons. Good solar materials are defect-free so that electrons are not lost.

Efficient current collection

Free electrons in a semiconductor must flow in one direction to generate a current. Adding layers to either side of the solar cell that respectively transmit and block electrons ensures that free electrons are collected as current.

Why are we excited about 2D semiconductors?

High absorption per unit thickness

2D transition metal dichalcogenides have exceptionally high absorption even in very thin layers. They can absorb 10 to 100 times as much light per thickness as traditional semiconductors such as Silicon or Gallium Arsenide.

Pristine interfaces

Since 2D semiconductors naturally occur in a layered form, it’s easy to stack them on top of each other—similar to legos—to create the stacked structure necessary for a full solar cell. In contrast, 3D semiconductors often incur defects at interfaces between materials.
